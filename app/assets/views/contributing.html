Contributing to Z Brain

Registering new data to the Z Brain Atlas

Option 1: Get us to register your data


To register your new line or stain, you will need to acquire high-quality 2-channel confocal stacks of your line.
Your new label will need to be co-stained with anti-ERK (p44/42 MAPK (Erk1/2) (L34F12) Mouse mAb #4696, http://www.cellsignal.com/products/primary-antibodies/4696.
If you are registering another mouse IgG1 antibody and so can’t easily use ERK as a stain, you can use another label currently contained in the database. For this we recommend you use Anti-Znp1. See below for our recommended staining protocol.

Image stacks should be acquired on a confocal microscope, using a setup and settings as close to the following as possible:

  • x/y/z resolution = 0.798/0.798/2um
  • 20x 1.0NA water dipping objective
  • Fish are mounted in low-melting point agarose, imaged with dorsal side closest to the objective (up on an upright scope)

Stacks should be acquired to cover the entire brain from dorsal to ventral surface, and from the tip of the nose to a few somites into the spinal cord (same coverage as Z-Brain). To do this you can acquire two image tiles, which can be stitched using the ‘pairwise stitching’ function in FIJI.
Once you have the stacks, open them in FIJI. Split the channels of the 2-channel stack (Image -> Color -> Split Channels). Save each channel separately as an “nrrd” file (File -> Save As -> nrrd). The image stack of the ERK stain should be named as: ThisFishName_01.nrrd. Your new label file should have the same name, but replace ‘_01.nrrd’ with ‘_02.nrrd’. And so on, if more than 2 labels.
Open the saved nrrd files in FIJI to make sure that the metadata is correct. Go to Image->Properties, and ensure that ‘pixel width’, ‘pixel height’ and ‘pixel depth’ are the physical dimensions of the voxels in your image. These must be correct for the registration to work optimally.

Send the image files to me (zebrafishbrain@gmail.com). Dropbox, Google Drive, or physically mailing a hard drive are a few options. The more stacks of different fish you can send the better, in order to get the best representation of it in the Z-Brain. We will need a minimum of 5 fish, but aim for 20. We will register all the fish to the Z-Brain, and calculate the mean-across-fish stack. We are happy to do this for you, with the understanding that the data will eventually be added to the Z-Brain database. This could be done immediately, or after you publish your reagent.

Before sending the full dataset, please send me some example image slices of your stack to ensure the quality will be high enough for registration.


Option 2: Register data yourself

To register data we use CMTK. I think the easiest way to get this running is to use the graphical interfaces built by the Jefferis lab. Detailed instructions on warping data are available here, and here (note, these tools were originally made to register fly brains, but they also do an excellent job registering fish brains). For typical registrations we pass these parameters to the registration GUI/munger: -awr 0102 -X 52 -C 8 -G 80 -R 3 -A '--accuracy 0.4' -W '--accuracy 1.6'. The tERK reference brain can be found here: Reference Brain
We would appreciate it if you could provide us with the registered data to be included in the Z-Brain database. Ideally, this could be done in conjunction with publication of the reagent.


Contributing to the segmentation effort

The segmentation of the Z-Brain into anatomical regions was done manually by us, and partially edited through consultation with Mario Wullimann. Performing a true and accurate high-resolution 3D segmentation of all of the neuroanatomical regions is an immense task, which is certainly not fully realized at present. We hope that you will help us to improve it by adding new labels (above) and by contributing new and/or updated region definitions. These regions are represented by binary image stacks, with 1’s defining the region.

To create a new regional definition, you can use the ZBrainViewer. With the dataset you will use to guide segmentation open and in ‘coronal’ (default) view, click ‘r’ to begin drawing a new region. Instructions for how to draw the region will be displayed.

Draw the outline of the region in the current plane, and then move to a new plane and again draw the relevant outline. Once you have drawn a sufficient number of planes to define the region, press ‘i’ to interpolate any undrawn planes and complete the 3D mask. You can check the accuracy of your drawing by scrolling through the planes after interpolating.

Pressing ‘s’ will prompt you to give the region a name, and save to disk in a separate file called ‘NewAnatomyRegion—YourRegion’sName.mat’. Then, pressing ‘w’ will integrate your new region into your local version of the Z-Brain database.

Email the ‘NewAnatomyRegion--...’ file, along with a description of what the region is, and what defines it, to me. I will vet it for inclusion in the Z-Brain.

tERK staining protocol

1) Fixation

  • Fix larvae in 4% PFA in PBS + 0.25% Triton (PBT), overnight (O/N) at 4⁰C.
  • Wash in PBT 3x 5min

2) Antigen Retrieval

(optional, improves signal of some antibodies like Znp1, but doesn’t affect tERK staining substantially, Inoue et al., Plos One, 2011)

  • Incubate in 150mM TrisHCl pH 9.0 for 5min at RT
  • Transfer to 70⁰C water bath for 15min
  • Wash with PBT 2x 5 min

3) Permeabilization

  • Incubate larvae in 0.05% Trypsin-EDTA on ice. Optimal time will depend on stage. I use 45min for 6 dpf larvae
  • Wash 3x in PBT (I do 2 quick washes to make sure I get all of the trypsin out, and then wash for 10 min with PBT)

4) Blocking

  • 1 hour in 2% Normal Goat Serum, 1% BSA, 1% DMSO in PBT
  • Only use NGS if the no primary antibodies are made in goat.

5) Antibodies

  • Dilute the ERK antibody 1:500 in 1%BSA, 1%DMSO in PBT, and incubate O/N at 4⁰C
  • Wash 3X 15 min in PBT at RT
  • Incubate in 2nd AB in 1%BSA, 1%DMSO in PBT, O/N at 4C
  • Wash 3X 15 min in PBT at RT
  • Mount for imaging in LMP Agarose, mount as close to the imaging surface as possible to minimize light scattering by the agarose
